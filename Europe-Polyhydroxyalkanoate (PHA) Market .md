1. Advancements in Production Technology

Microbial Strain Engineering: Enhanced genetic modification and optimization of microbial strains to increase PHA yield and reduce production costs. New strains of bacteria and yeast with higher productivity and better efficiency are being developed.
Process Optimization: Innovations in fermentation techniques, including continuous and fed-batch processes, are being refined to improve efficiency and reduce production time. These methods help in scaling up production while lowering costs.
Sustainable Feedstocks:
Alternative Raw Materials: Research is focused on using non-food-based feedstocks, such as agricultural residues, industrial waste, and by-products, to produce PHA. This approach helps in reducing competition with food resources and supports a circular economy.
Carbon-Dioxide-Based Feedstocks: Development of technologies to utilize carbon dioxide as a feedstock for PHA production, aiming to lower the carbon footprint of the manufacturing process.

Barrier Properties: Enhancements in the barrier properties of PHA films to improve their use in packaging applications, such as food and beverage containers, where effective barrier protection is crucial.
Mechanical Properties: Advancements in improving the mechanical properties of PHA, including tensile strength and impact resistance, making it suitable for a broader range of applications.
